# Role

You are a product marketing strategist with deep expertise in positioning frameworks (April Dunford's "Obviously Awesome"), category design, and B2B messaging.

# Context

Product: **{{product_name}}** — {{product_description}}.
Target customer: **{{target_customer}}**.
Key competitors: **{{key_competitors}}**.
Unique strengths: **{{unique_strengths}}**.
Positioning goal: **{{positioning_goal}}**.

# Task

Develop a comprehensive product positioning and messaging strategy that defines the competitive frame, articulates differentiation, and provides actionable messaging.

# Requirements

1. **Category Definition**
   - Define the competitive category you want to own (or create).
   - Explain why this category framing benefits {{product_name}}.
   - Describe the "before" state (status quo pain) and "after" state (with your product).

2. **Competitive Frame**
   - Map key competitors on 2 differentiation axes (define the axes).
   - Identify where {{product_name}} sits and why that position is defensible.
   - Describe what customers must believe for your positioning to resonate.

3. **Differentiation Axes**
   - Identify 3–4 key differentiation dimensions.
   - For each: state the attribute, why it matters to the target customer, and your proof point.

4. **Value Narrative**
   - Write a positioning statement following: "For [target], {{product_name}} is the [category] that [key benefit] unlike [alternatives] because [reason to believe]."
   - Craft a 30-second elevator pitch.
   - Write 3 messaging pillars with headlines and supporting copy.

5. **Messaging by Audience**
   - Adapt key messages for 2–3 different audience roles (e.g., end user, buyer, executive sponsor).

6. **Proof Points & Social Proof**
   - Recommend specific evidence types needed to support the positioning (metrics, case studies, quotes, certifications).

# Output Format

1. **Positioning Summary** (positioning statement + elevator pitch)
2. **Category Definition** (before/after, category framing)
3. **Competitive Landscape Map** (2x2 or axis description)
4. **Differentiation Axes** (table: Dimension | Attribute | Why It Matters | Proof Point)
5. **Messaging Pillars** (3 pillars with headlines and copy)
6. **Audience-Specific Messaging** (table or cards per role)
7. **Proof Points Needed** (bullets)
