# Customer Interview Guide: Notion Trial Downgrade Research

## Research Overview

| Detail | Value |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Objective** | Understand why power users downgrade to free tier after trial |
| **Participant Profile** | Users who trialed Team plan for 14+ days but did not convert |
| **Session Length** | 30 minutes |
| **Target Sample** | 8–12 participants |

## Screening Criteria

| Question | Ideal Answer | Disqualify If |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Did you use Notion's Team plan trial in the last 90 days? | Yes, within 90 days | Trial was more than 90 days ago |
| How many days did you actively use the trial? | 14+ days | Used fewer than 7 days |
| Did you use Notion with at least one other person during the trial? | Yes, 2+ collaborators | Solo use only |
| Are you the person who decided not to upgrade? | Yes, primary decision-maker | Someone else made the call |
| Do you currently use any project management or docs tool? | Yes, actively using alternatives | No current tooling |

## Interview Script

### Section 1: Warm-Up (3 minutes)

1. "Thanks for joining — I really appreciate your time. I'm [Name], a product researcher at Notion. There are no right or wrong answers here. We're genuinely trying to learn from your experience."
2. "This conversation will take about 30 minutes. Is it okay if I record this for internal notes? Nothing will be shared publicly."
3. "To start, can you tell me a bit about your role and what a typical workday looks like for you?"

### Section 2: Perceived Value (8 minutes)

4. "Walk me through how you first started using the Notion Team plan. What prompted the trial?"
   - *Probe:* "What were you hoping it would solve that your current setup didn't?"
   - *Probe:* "Was there a specific moment when you thought 'this could really work'?"
5. "What features or workflows did you use most during the trial?"
   - *Probe:* "How did those compare to what you expected going in?"
6. "When you think about the value you got from the Team plan versus the free tier, what stands out?"

### Section 3: Collaboration Friction (7 minutes)

7. "Tell me about a time during the trial when you tried to collaborate with someone in Notion. How did that go?"
   - *Probe:* "What was smooth? What felt clunky or frustrating?"
8. "Did your teammates adopt Notion during the trial, or was it mostly you?"
   - *Probe:* "What do you think made them engage — or not engage — with it?"
9. "Was there a moment when collaboration broke down or didn't meet your expectations?"

### Section 4: Pricing Sensitivity (7 minutes)

10. "At what point did you start thinking about whether to continue with the paid plan?"
    - *Probe:* "What factors were you weighing?"
11. "How did the price of the Team plan feel relative to what you were getting?"
    - *Probe:* "Was there a price point where you would have said yes without hesitation?"
12. "Did you compare Notion's pricing with any alternatives? What did that look like?"

### Section 5: Alternative Tools (3 minutes)

13. "What tools are you using now instead of Notion's Team plan?"
    - *Probe:* "What does that tool do better — or worse — than Notion did during the trial?"
14. "If you could take one feature from Notion and put it into your current tool, what would it be?"

### Section 6: Closing (2 minutes)

15. "Is there anything about your trial experience that we haven't covered but that felt important to you?"
16. "If you could change one thing about Notion that would have made you upgrade, what would it be?"
17. "Thanks so much — this has been incredibly helpful. We'll send a small thank-you gift to your email."

## Interviewer Tips

* **Embrace silence.** After the participant answers, wait 3–5 seconds before responding. They often elaborate with the most valuable insights during the pause.
* **Mirror, don't lead.** Repeat their last few words as a question rather than suggesting answers. "It felt too expensive?" instead of "Was it the pricing?"
* **Stay curious, not confirming.** Avoid nodding enthusiastically at answers that match your hypothesis. Treat every response as new information.
* **Track energy shifts.** Note when participants lean in, hesitate, or change tone — these moments often signal strong emotions worth probing.
* **Separate observation from interpretation.** Write down what they said, not what you think they meant. Interpretation happens in synthesis.
